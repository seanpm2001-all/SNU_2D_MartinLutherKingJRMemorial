Welcome to the SNU Martin Luther King Jr Memorial page

Martin Luther King Jr was a prominent civil rights activist during the civil rights movement in the United States of America in the 1950s and 1960s. This page is a memorial for his efforts.

Martin Luther King Jr. was from Atlanta Georgia. During the Civil Rights movement, he made prominent peaceful protests, not fighting back with violence.

Incidents with the FBI

During Dr. Martin Luther King Jrs. leadership, the FBI was doing things to try and bring him down. For example, the FBI wiretapped his phone calls constantly [source needed].

In 1964, an anonymous package was sent to Coretta Scott and Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. which contained a threatening letter, and an incriminating tape. [source needed] even though it was anonymous, Martin Luther King assumed it was from the FBI.

He thought the letter was telling him to commit suicide, others think it was a letter to tell him to step down from leadership, and or not accept the Nobel price [source needed]

An almost unredacted copy of the letter sent to Martin Luther King Jr. credit Wikimedia commons

Montgomery bus boycott



Rosa Parks with Martin Luther King Jr in 1955

The Montgomery bus boycott was a protest led by Martin Luther King over segregation on city buses. One of the major events that sparked the protest was when Rosa Parks was jailed and beaten for refusing to give up her seat for a white man.

The Montgomery bus boycott lasted from December 5th 1955 to December 20th 1956 (1 year 15 days) the protest was successful. 75% of the bus riders were in the protest, and after the bus system suffered, a decision was made to end segregation on bus systems in the south.

The campaign was successful, and ended segregation on bus systems in the South of America.

This campaign helped make Martin Luther King stand out more in his civil rights quest.

Assassination

Martin Luther King Jr. was assasinated on April 4, 1968 at 6:01 p.m. He was shot by James Earl Ray on the second floor balcony of the Lorraine Motel. He died of a fatal gunshot wound at 7:05 pm after emergency chest surgery. He was 39 years old. [source needed]This was before his final campaign: the poor people campaign.

James Earl Ray was sentenced to 99 years in prison, which was pretty much a life sentence. James died in prison in the year 1998 to hepatitis [source needed]

Legacy

Martin Luther King Jr. left behind a massive historical legacy in American human rights. A holiday was made for him in 1971, and was made official by President Ronald Reagan in 1983, however it wasn't celebrated in all 50 states until the year 2000. Martin Luther King day is celebrated on the 3rd Monday of every month.

Martin Luther King Jr. helped end racial segregation in the United States of America, and is known as a famous figure in human rights.

Starting in 2020, Martin Luther King Jr. is planned to be the face on the back of the $5.00 bill (5 dollar bill)

Inspirations

Martin Luther King Jr. got his inspirations from other civil rights activists. One such activist was Mahatma Gandhi. Mahatma Gandhi was an indian peaceful protester who didn't use violence to protest.
